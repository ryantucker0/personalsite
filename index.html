Ryan Tucker

At my core, is a strong drive to enable both myself, and others to succeed. I love technology and what it allows a motivated person to do. I am studying Information, Communication, and Technology, to build a strong technical background, along with a powerful ability to communicate.

I am a hard working, motivated individual who will make sure the job gets done. I have experience with front-end web development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Bootstrap) as well as object oriented programming (C++ and Java.) On top of that, I have project management experience as a co-founder and lead coordinator of HackFSU and I was Vice President of Technole.

Let's work on something together. Contact me at ryantucker0@gmail.com.

Technole

Technole is a Recognized Student Organization at Florida State University focused on teaching and contributing to technology in the Tallahassee area.

Vice President

As Vice President, I looked over all branches of the organization including marketing, community outreach, LyteHacks, HackFSU, hackathon travel, Study Sesh, and whatever else our impressive members wanted to create. This role allowed me to sharpen my management skills as well as work with some of the most talented students at FSU. Because of this experience I have become a better leader, learned to work in a strong dynamic environment, and learned how to help others to achieve.



Other Positions

Before becoming Vice President, I worked as head of media production and I am a cofounder of LyteHacks and HackFSU. My time in Technole taught me many valuable things including how to work on a fast paced team with others, many technical skills including front-end web development, and extreme time management.

HackFSU

Co-Founder and Lead Coordinator

HackFSU is a hackathon hosted by students at Florida State University. I, along with a small team, founded the event, the first of which was held in April of 2014. We have worked to find upwards of $25,000 from sponsors, engaged in outreach to maximize attendance, and I was the initial developer of our website: hackfsu.com. This experience has shown me first hand what it is like to throw a major event - something that is not done easily. I was able to increase my HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills, grow an idea into something tangible and successful, and leave a legacy that will live on past when I graduate.

Humana

IT Intern

I worked as a Business Analyst for a development team under Rules Guidance Exchange. Our team worked on a redesign for an internal Business Rules Management Tool. My role on the team was to maintain contact with our product owner and prototype the product in order to ensure the fulfillment of requirements. I would then relay that information back to others on my team for continuation. I gained skills with database management, Axure RP Pro, and agile methodology.

Founder of Humana Intern Hackathon

I also created Humana's first ever intern hackathon which involved leading a planning team to make sure the event was coordinated properly. The interns produced multiple projects that gained interest throughout the company. The event was a major success and was continued after I left the organization.
